Getting Started

Pick your niche. Specialist > generalist. "I help [who] do [what]."
Build a portfolio. No clients yet? Do spec work or free projects.
Start on platforms. Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal, Freelancer. Build reviews.
Then go direct. Higher rates, no platform fees. LinkedIn, referrals.

Setting Rates

Hourly: Good for starting. Track time carefully.
Project-based: Better margins if efficient. Define scope clearly.
Value-based: Charge based on client value, not your time. Advanced.

Rule: Double what you'd accept as an employee (you pay own taxes, benefits).

Finding Clients

Referrals are gold. Ask happy clients to refer others.
LinkedIn outreach. Personalized messages, not spam.
Content marketing. Blog, social, YouTube. Demonstrate expertise.
Cold email. Research, personalize, offer value.
Local businesses. Often underserved. Walk in and pitch.

Client Management

Contracts always. Scope, timeline, payment terms, revisions.
50% upfront. Especially new clients. Protects you.
Over-communicate. Updates, progress, questions. No surprises.
Set boundaries. Work hours, response time, revision limits.
